Types of Digital Products on Shopify

Shopify can sell almost any type of digital product, but the delivery mechanism and protection requirements vary by type. Understanding what category your product falls into helps determine which app and configuration is right for your store.

Downloadable Files

The most straightforward category. Customers pay and immediately receive a download link via email and in their order confirmation. Common examples include PDF guides and ebooks, Canva or Photoshop templates, SVG and digital art files, music tracks, sound packs, and Lightroom presets. File security here means expiring download links and download attempt limits.

Software & License Keys

Software, plugins, and tools require license key management on top of file delivery. Each customer needs a unique key that activates their copy. Apps like SendOwl support automatic key generation and delivery from a pre-uploaded pool, while more complex licensing requires a custom Shopify app or third-party integration.

Course Access & Memberships

Online courses and membership content are best delivered as a URL or access credentials rather than a direct download. This is where the line between Shopify as a storefront and a dedicated LMS (Learning Management System) becomes important. Shopify handles the transaction; the LMS handles the content delivery and student experience.

Digital Product Categories at a Glance

  • PDF documents — ebooks, guides, workbooks, planners
  • Design assets — templates, mockups, fonts, icon packs, illustrations
  • Audio files — music, podcasts, samples, sound effects, meditation tracks
  • Video files — stock footage, tutorials (downloaded, not streamed)
  • Software & tools — plugins, scripts, apps, spreadsheets
  • Course access — LMS login credentials or platform redirect links
  • Membership access — community, subscription content, exclusive portals

File Protection & License Management

File protection is not about making your products impossible to pirate — it's about making casual sharing sufficiently inconvenient to protect your revenue from your actual customer base. A motivated pirate will always find a way; your protection system is aimed at the 98% of people who would share a file out of convenience, not malice.

Expiring Download Links

Set download links to expire 48–72 hours after purchase and limit to 3–5 download attempts. This prevents a buyer from sharing their purchase link on a forum for others to download weeks later. Sky Pilot and SendOwl both support configurable expiry. Always include a way for legitimate customers to re-request their download — a simple support email or automated self-service portal prevents frustration.

PDF Watermarking

Sky Pilot's PDF stamping feature dynamically inserts the buyer's name and email address into every page of a PDF before delivery. This creates a social deterrent — people are far less likely to share a document with their personal information embedded throughout it. For premium guides priced above $50, this is a worthwhile layer of protection.

License Keys for Software

Upload a pool of pre-generated license keys to SendOwl. With each purchase, a unique key is automatically emailed to the buyer. The keys can be validated against your activation server, limiting installations to a defined number per license. This model is standard for Shopify plugins, scripts, and desktop software sold through Shopify as a marketplace front-end.

Course & Membership Integration

The decision between keeping your course content on Shopify versus a dedicated LMS is one of the most consequential architecture choices for digital educators. There is no universally correct answer — it depends on the complexity of your course structure, the student experience you want to deliver, and your budget.

When to Keep on Shopify (Sky Pilot)

Sky Pilot allows you to create gated content pages directly within your Shopify store, accessible only to customers who have purchased the relevant product. This works well for straightforward content libraries, downloadable resources, and simple video bundles. There's no separate platform for students to log into — they access content through their Shopify customer account. The trade-off is limited progress tracking, no quizzes, and no certificate generation.

When to Use a Dedicated LMS

If your course includes quizzes, completion certificates, cohort learning, drip content schedules, discussion forums, or complex prerequisites, a dedicated platform like Teachable, Thinkific, or Kajabi is worth the additional cost. Use Shopify as the checkout engine — sell enrollment through Shopify and use Zapier or a custom webhook to automatically enroll the buyer on your LMS platform. This gives you best-of-breed checkout conversion alongside best-of-breed learning experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to charge VAT on digital products? +
Yes, in most jurisdictions. EU customers must be charged VAT at their local rate for digital goods regardless of where your business is located — this is the EU VAT on digital services rule. UK, Australia, Norway, and many other countries have similar rules. Shopify Tax handles this automatically if configured correctly with proper tax settings for digital goods, but always verify before going live and consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
What file size limits exist for digital products on Shopify? +
Shopify's native digital file upload limit is 5GB per file. Sky Pilot supports up to 10GB and hosts files on its own CDN, which improves download speeds globally. SendOwl and Fetchapp have lower limits on base plans. For very large files like full video courses, hosting on Amazon S3 or a video CDN and delivering a secure streaming link is more practical and cost-effective than attempting direct file downloads.
What should my refund policy be for digital downloads? +
Digital products are generally non-refundable once downloaded or accessed, and most consumer protection laws allow this exception when clearly disclosed at checkout. State clearly in your policy that no refunds are issued after the file is accessed or downloaded. Always offer refunds for files that fail to deliver, are corrupted, or materially differ from their description — this is both legally required and protects your brand reputation.
How do I prevent customers from sharing my digital files? +
Use time-limited download links that expire after 48–72 hours or after 3–5 download attempts. Add PDF watermarking with the buyer's name and email on every page. For software, use license key systems that limit activations per key. No system prevents all piracy, but these measures eliminate casual sharing — which accounts for the vast majority of revenue leakage in digital product businesses.
Can I sell both physical and digital products from the same Shopify store? +
Yes, and many successful stores do this effectively. A photography store might sell physical prints alongside digital download packs. A music producer might sell physical merch alongside sample packs. The key configuration requirement is ensuring your digital delivery app only triggers for digital products — not physical orders that still need shipping labels. Sky Pilot handles this automatically by attaching delivery to specific products, not all orders.
