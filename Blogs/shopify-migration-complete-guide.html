The core of Shopify migration is transferring your WooCommerce data accurately. This section covers what data can be migrated and how to map it correctly to Shopify's structure.

Data Migration Compatibility

Products & Variants Fully Supported Direct migration with all attributes Custom fields may need manual recreation
Product Categories Fully Supported Maps to Shopify Collections Hierarchy may need adjustment
Customer Data Fully Supported Includes addresses and order history Passwords require reset
Order History Fully Supported Complete order data migration Order statuses may need mapping
Blog Posts Fully Supported Content, images, and metadata SEO settings need recreation
Coupons & Discounts Limited Support Basic coupons migrate Complex rules need manual setup

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does WooCommerce to Shopify migration take?
WooCommerce to Shopify migration typically takes 2-6 weeks depending on store complexity. Simple stores (under 1000 products) take 1-2 weeks, while complex stores with custom functionality may take 4-6 weeks including design recreation, data migration, testing, and SEO setup.
What data can be migrated from WooCommerce to Shopify?
You can migrate products (with variants, images, descriptions), customers (with order history and addresses), orders, categories (as collections), coupons, reviews, and blog posts. Custom fields and certain plugins may require manual recreation or custom development in Shopify.
Will I lose SEO rankings during Shopify migration?
Proper migration with 301 redirects preserves SEO rankings. We map all important URLs, set up proper redirects, recreate meta tags, and ensure search engines can crawl the new site properly. Temporary ranking fluctuations are normal but typically recover within 2-4 weeks with proper SEO preservation techniques.
How much does WooCommerce to Shopify migration cost?
Migration costs range from $1,999-$9,999 depending on store complexity, data volume, custom features, and design recreation needs. Basic migrations start at $1,999, while complex migrations with custom development can cost up to $9,999. This includes data migration, theme setup, SEO preservation, testing, and 60 days of support.
Can custom WooCommerce functionality be recreated in Shopify?
Most custom WooCommerce functionality can be recreated in Shopify using apps, custom development, or Shopify's built-in features. Complex custom functionality may require custom app development or alternative approaches. We analyze all custom features during the planning phase to ensure proper recreation.
Will customers need to create new accounts after migration?
Customer accounts migrate with order history and addresses, but passwords cannot be transferred for security reasons. Customers will need to reset their passwords using the "forgot password" feature. We provide clear communication templates to inform customers about this process during migration.
What happens to my WordPress blog during migration?
Blog posts can be migrated to Shopify's built-in blog feature, maintaining content, images, and basic formatting. However, complex WordPress blog functionality may be limited. Some businesses choose to keep WordPress for blogging and integrate it with Shopify, depending on their content marketing strategy.
Is there any downtime during the migration process?
Professional migration minimizes downtime to just the time needed for DNS propagation (typically 2-24 hours). We build the entire Shopify store on a staging domain, test thoroughly, then switch domains when ready. Your WooCommerce store remains fully functional until the switch is complete.
