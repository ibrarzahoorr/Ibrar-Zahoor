Australian Ecommerce Specifics

An Australian Shopify store has a handful of local requirements that a developer without AU experience commonly gets wrong or skips:

  • GST at 10%: once your business crosses the AUD 75,000 GST registration threshold, Shopify Tax needs to be configured to include GST correctly in displayed prices (Australian consumers expect tax-inclusive pricing, unlike US stores that add tax at checkout) and your invoices need your ABN listed, which usually means a small theme or checkout customisation, not just a settings toggle.
  • AfterPay and Zip: buy-now-pay-later isn't a nice-to-have in Australia — Afterpay and Zip both started here, and AU shoppers expect to see them at checkout and on product pages as a payment badge. Getting these installed, correctly styled, and showing accurate instalment breakdowns on product pages measurably affects conversion in a way that US-style Klarna/Affirm placement doesn't fully replicate.
  • Australian Consumer Law (ACL): AU has stricter statutory return/refund obligations than most markets — "change of mind" policies need to be clearly distinguished from ACL-guaranteed remedies (faulty goods) in your returns page and checkout copy, which is a content and sometimes app-logic task.
  • Shipping reality: Australia Post is expensive and slow relative to US/UK carrier costs, and the country's geography means "same state" shipping (Sydney to Melbourne) can take longer than international shoppers expect. Apps like Sendle or regional carrier integrations are common workarounds worth planning for at build time.

The Timezone Gap — An Honest Breakdown

Sydney runs on AEST (UTC+10) or AEDT (UTC+11) during Australian daylight saving (October to April). Pakistan is UTC+5 year-round. That puts Sydney 5-6 hours ahead of Pakistan — the reverse of the UK/UAE situation, and genuinely the toughest overlap of any market I regularly work with.

In plain terms: Sydney's 9am start is already 3-4am in Lahore. A standard Pakistani working day (9am-6pm PKT) lines up with Sydney's early afternoon through late evening (2pm-11pm AEST) — meaning there's real overlap with the back half of an Australian business day and the evening, but not with an AU team's morning.

How this actually gets handled:
  1. Standard schedule: for most ongoing work, my normal hours give you live overlap through your Sydney afternoon and evening — enough for a daily check-in call and same-day responses to anything flagged by early-to-mid afternoon your time.
  2. Shifted schedule for launches: for a store launch, a migration cutover, or a high-stakes go-live, I'll shift to an early Lahore start (around dawn) to be live during your Sydney morning too — this isn't the default, but it's available when the project needs it.
  3. Async by design: detailed written specs, Loom recordings of finished work, and a shared task board (Trello/Asana/ClickUp) mean work keeps moving through the hours we're not both online, rather than stalling until the next overlap window.

If your business genuinely needs someone reachable during your 9-5 every single day without exception, be upfront about that in the scoping call — it may mean a shifted-hours arrangement priced slightly differently, or it may mean a hybrid setup with local support for real-time needs and me handling the deeper technical build.

Frequently Asked Questions

Isn't a 5-6 hour reverse gap a dealbreaker?

Not for most Shopify project work. It rules out "always-on, instant Slack reply" expectations during your morning, but for scoped builds, migrations, and retainers with a daily sync, it's manageable — and I'd rather tell you that plainly than promise a timezone convenience that doesn't exist.

Can you actually configure AfterPay and Zip correctly, not just install the app?

Yes — that includes product-page instalment messaging, correct badge placement, and making sure the checkout flow doesn't create friction between AfterPay/Zip and your other payment methods, which is where a lot of default installs fall short.

What happens if something breaks outside our overlap window?

For genuinely urgent issues (site down, checkout broken), I check messages outside standard hours for active clients and will jump on a fix even if it's outside the "normal" overlap — that's part of how the working relationship stays trustworthy despite the timezone gap.
