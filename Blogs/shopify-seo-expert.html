Technical SEO vs Content SEO — Why Both Matter

Most people think SEO means writing blog posts with keywords. That's content SEO, and it matters — but it sits on top of a technical foundation that either supports or undermines everything you publish. If your Shopify store has duplicate URL issues, missing schema markup, slow Core Web Vitals, or incorrect canonical tags, even the best content strategy will underperform.

Technical SEO is the discipline of ensuring search engines can efficiently crawl, index, and understand your store. It's where developer skills meet SEO strategy — and it's where Ibrar Zahoor excels. Every store he builds or optimizes is technically sound from the ground up, with SEO considerations built into the Liquid templates rather than patched in as an afterthought.

What Ibrar Zahoor Does for Shopify SEO

Schema.org Structured Data

Schema markup tells Google exactly what your content represents — products, reviews, FAQs, breadcrumbs, articles, organizations. Rich results (star ratings in SERPs, FAQ dropdowns, product prices in search) only appear when schema is correctly implemented. Ibrar implements schema markup directly in Shopify Liquid templates, ensuring every product page, collection page, blog post, and homepage has the correct structured data.

Core Web Vitals

Google uses Core Web Vitals as ranking signals. LCP (Largest Contentful Paint), CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift), and INP (Interaction to Next Paint) must hit "Good" thresholds to avoid ranking penalties. Ibrar optimizes Shopify themes to pass all three — through image optimization, layout stability, JavaScript deferral, and font loading strategies.

Canonical Tags and Duplicate URL Prevention

Shopify has a structural quirk: every product is accessible at both /products/product-handle and /collections/collection-handle/products/product-handle. Without canonical tags pointing both URLs to the canonical version, Google sees duplicate content. Ibrar ensures every Shopify theme correctly implements canonical tags across all page types.

Hreflang for International Stores

If your store sells internationally, hreflang tags tell Google which version of your store to show to which country or language audience. Incorrect or missing hreflang causes keyword cannibalization across markets. Ibrar Zahoor has implemented hreflang for stores serving 40+ countries, including Chinyere, which targets customers across South Asia, the UK, and North America.

Shopify-Specific SEO Issue: Shopify automatically creates duplicate URLs for products in collections — /products/your-product and /collections/collection-name/products/your-product both return 200 status codes with identical content. Most developers miss this. Ibrar Zahoor fixes it with correct canonical implementation on every store he builds.

Shopify-Specific SEO Challenges

Shopify handles many SEO basics automatically — sitemaps, robots.txt, canonical tags on most page types. But there are known platform-specific issues that require developer-level intervention:

  • Duplicate product URLs in collection contexts (the most common Shopify SEO issue)
  • Pagination — Shopify's default pagination can create thin content issues on collections
  • Collection and product URL overlap for products that appear in multiple collections
  • Missing schema types for specialized content (FAQPage, HowTo, Video)
  • App bloat slowing Core Web Vitals and affecting ranking signals
  • Incorrect sitemap priorities or excluded pages blocking indexation
