Shopify Markets — What It Is and Why It Matters

Shopify Markets is Shopify's built-in international commerce platform that allows you to sell to multiple countries from a single Shopify store. Launched in 2021 and significantly expanded since, Shopify Markets handles the most complex aspects of international e-commerce: currency conversion, local payment methods, market-specific pricing, domain and subdomain routing per market, and localization of checkout and transactional emails.

Before Shopify Markets, merchants who wanted to sell internationally either had to run multiple separate Shopify stores (expensive and complex to manage) or use third-party apps (limited and often unreliable). Markets centralizes everything: you manage all your markets from a single admin, and the storefront handles the market-specific experience automatically based on visitor geolocation.

Each Market in Shopify is a group of countries or regions that shares a currency, pricing, and configuration. You can create as many markets as needed — for example: a "UK & Ireland" market with GBP pricing, a "Europe" market with EUR pricing, a "North America" market with USD pricing, and a "MENA" market with AED or SAR pricing. Within each market, you configure specific pricing, payment methods, and shipping rates.

Automatic Currency Conversion

With Shopify Payments enabled, Shopify automatically converts your store's base currency prices to each market's currency using live exchange rates updated daily. Customers in France see prices in EUR, customers in the UK see GBP, customers in Canada see CAD — all without any manual price entry. The currency selector in your header updates automatically based on geolocation, and customers can manually switch if needed.

For more control, Shopify Markets also supports fixed local prices per market — you set the exact price in each currency rather than relying on automatic conversion. This is valuable when you want pricing that reflects local market positioning (e.g., a product that costs $50 USD might be priced at £45 GBP rather than the exact converted £39.50). Ibrar configures the right approach based on your pricing strategy and how many SKUs you're managing.

Geolocation & Automatic Market Detection

Shopify Markets uses visitor IP geolocation to automatically detect the visitor's country and redirect them (or display the appropriate currency/language) for the correct market. On themes that support it, a "country/region selector" popup appears for first-time visitors confirming their market. Ibrar customizes this selector to match your brand design and configures the geolocation logic to avoid incorrect detections (VPN users, corporate IP ranges, etc.).

Local Payment Methods Per Region

Payment method preference varies dramatically by region. US customers expect credit cards and PayPal. German customers prefer SEPA bank transfers and SOFORT. Dutch customers use iDEAL. Brazilian customers use Boleto Bancário. Middle Eastern customers often prefer COD (cash on delivery). Offering only credit cards to a German audience loses a significant portion of potential sales. Shopify Payments supports local payment methods natively in supported countries — Ibrar configures these per market to maximize checkout completion rates.

hreflang Tags for International SEO

When you have the same or similar content targeting multiple countries and languages, search engines need hreflang tags to understand which version to show to which users. Without proper hreflang implementation, Google may index the wrong version of your store for international searches — or treat your market-specific URLs as duplicate content.

Shopify handles hreflang tags automatically for Shopify Markets if you're using subdomains or subdirectories per market (e.g., uk.yourstore.com or yourstore.com/en-gb). Ibrar verifies the hreflang implementation in Google Search Console after setup and fixes any signal mismatches that can occur with complex market configurations.

Tax Rules by Country

International tax compliance is one of the most complex aspects of cross-border commerce. VAT in the EU, GST in Australia, GST/HST in Canada, and varying tax rules across the US all need to be handled correctly. Incorrect tax collection creates legal risk and accounting headaches. Shopify Markets includes automatic tax calculation for major markets via Shopify Tax. For markets not covered natively, Ibrar configures manual tax rules and recommends integration with Avalara or TaxJar for complex US sales tax compliance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Shopify support multiple currencies? +
Yes. Shopify supports multiple currencies through Shopify Markets. With Shopify Payments, prices are automatically converted to the customer's local currency using daily exchange rates. You can also set fixed local prices per market. Customers see prices in their local currency, and checkout processes in that currency. Currency conversion fees may apply depending on your Shopify plan.
How do I set up Shopify Markets? +
Setting up Shopify Markets involves: navigating to Settings > Markets in your Shopify admin, creating markets for each region (grouping countries), configuring currencies and pricing per market, setting up local payment methods via Shopify Payments, configuring shipping rates per market, setting tax rules per region, and optionally setting up subdomains or subdirectories for market-specific URLs. Ibrar handles the complete Markets setup including SEO configuration and testing.
What is hreflang for Shopify? +
Hreflang is an HTML attribute that tells search engines which version of a page is intended for which language and/or country. For Shopify stores with multiple international markets (e.g., a UK version and a US version), hreflang tags tell Google that yourstore.com/en-gb should appear in UK search results while yourstore.com should appear in US results. Shopify Markets handles hreflang automatically when using market-specific subdomains or subdirectories — Ibrar verifies and fixes the implementation via Google Search Console.
