Step-by-Step: Start Your Shopify Store in 2025

1

Sign Up for Shopify

Go to shopify.com and start your free 3-day trial (then $1/month for 3 months). Enter your email, create a password, and choose a store name. You can change the name later, so don't overthink it.

2

Answer the Setup Questions

Shopify asks: Are you just starting or already selling? What's your current revenue? This customizes your dashboard. Answer honestly — it doesn't lock you in.

3

Choose Your Shopify Theme

Go to Online Store → Themes → Explore Free Themes. For most beginners, Dawn (Shopify's default free theme) is fast, clean, and mobile-optimized. Premium themes cost $180–$350 and are worth it if you have specific design needs.

4

Add Your Products

Go to Products → Add Product. Fill in: Title, Description (200+ words with keywords), Price, Inventory quantity, Weight (for shipping), and upload high-quality photos (at least 3 per product, 2048x2048px).

5

Organize Products into Collections

Collections are categories. Create them in Products → Collections. Example: "Men's Clothing", "Under $50", "Summer Sale". Link collections to your navigation menu so customers can browse easily.

6

Set Up Payment Methods

Settings → Payments. Enable Shopify Payments (if available in your country) for the lowest transaction fees (0%). Also add PayPal as a backup. International stores should consider Stripe, which works in 40+ countries.

7

Configure Shipping Rates

Settings → Shipping and delivery. Set up: Free shipping over a threshold (e.g., free over $50 — this boosts conversion by 15–25%), flat-rate shipping, and carrier-calculated rates if needed.

8

Set Up Your Domain

Settings → Domains. Either buy a domain through Shopify ($14/year for .com) or connect an existing domain (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.). A custom domain makes your store look professional and trustworthy.

9

Configure Taxes

Settings → Taxes. Shopify auto-calculates US sales tax by state. For other countries, set the appropriate VAT or GST rates. Enable "Show price with tax" if selling to EU customers.

10

Add Essential Apps

Start with free apps only: Reviews (Judge.me or Loox), Email Marketing (Shopify Email or Klaviyo free tier), and SEO (Plug In SEO). Avoid installing too many apps at launch — each adds page load time.

11

Set Up Google Analytics & Search Console

Connect Google Analytics 4 via the Shopify admin (Online Store → Preferences → Google Analytics). Submit your sitemap (yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml) to Google Search Console for faster indexing.

12

Remove Password & Launch

Online Store → Preferences → Password protection → Disable password. Congratulations — your store is live! Share it on social media, tell your network, and run your first promotion.

Shopify Pricing Plans 2025

  • Basic ($29/month): 2 staff accounts, basic reports, 2.0% transaction fee without Shopify Payments — best for beginners
  • Shopify ($79/month): 5 staff accounts, professional reports, 1.0% transaction fee — best for growing stores
  • Advanced ($299/month): 15 staff accounts, advanced reports, 0.5% transaction fee — best for scaling businesses
  • Shopify Plus ($2,300+/month): Unlimited staff, custom checkout, B2B features — for enterprise brands

Pro Tip: Always pay annually to save 25% on any plan. The Basic plan is more than enough for your first 6–12 months.

5 Biggest Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Launching with only 1–2 products: Customers need to see a full store. Add at least 10–20 products before you drive traffic.
  2. Using the myshopify.com domain permanently: Get a custom domain within your first week — it's $14/year and builds trust immediately.
  3. Ignoring mobile: 70%+ of shoppers use mobile. Test every page on your phone before launching.
  4. Installing too many apps: Each app slows your store. Start with max 5 apps — add more only as needed.
  5. Skipping SEO: Write real product descriptions (200+ words), add alt text to images, and submit your sitemap to Google from day one.
