Shopify Wholesale Pricing — B2C and B2B From One Store

Running separate stores for retail and wholesale is expensive and inefficient. With the right Shopify setup, you can serve B2C customers and wholesale buyers from a single store — with different pricing, minimum orders, and checkout flows for each.

Shopify's Native B2B Features

Shopify has invested heavily in B2B functionality, particularly at the Plus tier. The Shopify Plus B2B suite — introduced as a native feature rather than a channel add-on — brings sophisticated wholesale capabilities directly into the admin without requiring third-party apps. Understanding what's native versus what requires apps determines your technology stack decisions.

Shopify Plus B2B: Company Profiles & Price Lists

At the Plus level, you create Company profiles for each wholesale account. A company can have multiple locations (different shipping addresses), multiple contacts with different roles, and a dedicated price list that sets either percentage discounts off retail or fixed wholesale prices per SKU. Payment terms (Net 30, Net 60, Net 90) are configured per company, and customers can place orders and receive invoices through their dedicated B2B portal — a white-labeled storefront experience separate from your retail store.

When Third-Party Apps Make More Sense

On Shopify Basic, Shopify, or Advanced plans, you cannot access the native B2B features. Third-party apps fill this gap effectively for most wholesale use cases. Apps like Wholesale Club (by Orbit), Wholesale Helper, and Boldly B2B all deliver tag-based pricing without requiring Plus. For most stores doing under $1M annual wholesale revenue, these apps provide 90% of the functionality at a fraction of the cost.

Customer Tag-Based Pricing Without Plus

The tag-based pricing model is the workhorse approach for wholesale on standard Shopify plans. When a customer is approved as a wholesale buyer, they receive a specific tag on their customer account — for example, "wholesale" or "tier-2-wholesale." Apps then apply price rules based on this tag, dynamically displaying the correct pricing when that customer is logged in.

Wholesale Club by Orbit

The most widely used wholesale app for non-Plus Shopify. It works by applying percentage or fixed discounts off your retail prices based on customer tags. You can create multiple wholesale tiers — Bronze at 20% off, Silver at 30% off, Gold at 40% off — each mapped to a different customer tag. The pricing displays correctly in the product listing, product page, cart, and order confirmation without any visible discount codes or awkward workarounds.

Locksmith for Access Control

Locksmith is the best solution for hiding prices or restricting entire sections of your store to wholesale customers. If you want retail visitors to see products but not prices (forcing them to log in for pricing), or want to hide wholesale-only products from retail customers entirely, Locksmith handles this with simple, powerful rules. It works at the template level, so performance is not affected.

Stack Tip: The most effective non-Plus wholesale setup combines Wholesale Club (for tag-based pricing) + Locksmith (for access control) + a custom wholesale application form built in Shopify's native form system or a tool like Typeform. Total monthly cost: under $60.

Minimum Order Quantities & Order Rules

MOQ enforcement is one of the most requested wholesale features and one of the trickier ones to implement cleanly. Shopify does not natively support minimum order quantities or per-product order minimums — these require app solutions or custom Liquid code.

Cart-Level MOQ

The simplest approach is a cart-level minimum order value — "wholesale orders require a $500 minimum." This is achievable with basic Shopify Scripts (Plus) or with Wholesale Club's built-in minimum order settings. Cart validation prevents checkout below the threshold and displays a clear message showing how much more is needed to qualify.

Per-Product and Per-Variant MOQ

More complex: requiring a minimum of 6 units of a specific product (e.g., sold as a 6-pack only). This requires either a custom app or specific Liquid theme modifications that check quantity inputs against defined minimums. Apps like Order Minimums and Order Limits by Limitsify handle this use case without custom development.

Tiered Pricing Breaks

Volume pricing tiers — 1–5 units at $10, 6–11 units at $8.50, 12+ units at $7 — are powerful wholesale conversion tools. Wholesale Club supports tiered pricing natively. The pricing table should be prominently displayed on the product page so wholesale buyers can immediately see the quantity economics.

Wholesale Registration Flow

The wholesale registration experience dramatically affects how many legitimate wholesale buyers actually convert. A clunky or unclear process sends potential accounts to your competitors. A well-designed wholesale application flow should take less than 3 minutes to complete and give applicants a clear expectation of review time.

Application Form Design

Use a dedicated page on your store (e.g., /pages/wholesale-application) with a form collecting: business name, contact name, email, phone, business type (retailer, distributor, etc.), website, tax/VAT number, estimated monthly order value, and how they heard about you. Keep it under 10 fields — every additional field reduces completion rates.

Approval & Tag Assignment

Manual review and tag assignment is fine when you're processing under 20 applications per week. You review the application, create or find the customer account, and manually add the appropriate wholesale tag. For higher volume, automate this with a Zapier flow — form submission creates a Shopify customer with a "pending-wholesale" tag, triggers an email to your team for review, and a one-click approval action adds the final "wholesale" pricing tag.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need Shopify Plus for wholesale pricing? +
No. Shopify Plus has the most powerful native B2B features including company profiles, price lists, and payment terms, but tag-based wholesale pricing works on all Shopify plans using third-party apps like Wholesale Club or Wholesale Helper. The main limitation on non-Plus plans is checkout customization — you cannot natively hide shipping methods or payment options from specific customer segments without Shopify Functions (Plus-only).
Can I hide retail prices from wholesale customers and vice versa? +
Yes. Using customer tags and apps like Locksmith or Wholesale Club, you can show wholesale prices only to tagged wholesale accounts while retail customers see standard pricing. Locksmith can hide prices entirely from non-logged-in visitors (showing "Login to see wholesale pricing"), hide entire collections from retail customers, or password-protect wholesale product pages to approved accounts only.
Can I offer Net 30 payment terms to wholesale buyers? +
On Shopify Plus, Net 30/60/90 payment terms are a native B2B feature — customers can place orders and pay by invoice within the agreed term period. On standard Shopify plans, simulate this with draft orders (manually send an invoice) or use an app like Apruve or Balance Commerce that integrates trade credit directly into the checkout experience for approved accounts.
How do volume discounts work differently from wholesale pricing? +
Volume discounts are quantity-based and typically available to all customers — buy 5 get 10% off, buy 10 get 20% off. Wholesale pricing is account-based — only approved wholesale accounts see the reduced prices regardless of quantity ordered. Many B2B stores combine both: wholesale accounts receive lower base prices AND get additional volume discount tiers on top, creating a powerful incentive structure for large orders.
Can wholesale customers order in different currencies? +
Yes, with Shopify Markets or a multi-currency setup. Shopify Plus B2B price lists can be set in specific currencies per company, allowing a German wholesale account to see EUR pricing while a US account sees USD pricing. For non-Plus stores, currency apps handle conversion but wholesale-specific pricing per currency requires custom Metafield or app configuration to avoid price discrepancies across currencies.
