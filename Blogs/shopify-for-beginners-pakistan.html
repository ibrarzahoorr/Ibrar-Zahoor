Shopify for Beginners in Pakistan 2026 — Complete Step-by-Step Guide

Everything a Pakistani beginner needs to know about starting a Shopify store in 2026 — free trial, payment setup, common mistakes, and when to call in a developer.

Is Shopify Right for You?

Before diving into setup steps, the honest question is: is Shopify the right platform for your situation? The answer depends on what you want to sell, your technical comfort level, and your budget.

Shopify is ideal if you want to sell physical products online without dealing with servers, if you plan to grow and need a scalable platform, or if you want a professional storefront that works on mobile, accepts multiple payment methods, and integrates with logistics providers. Shopify is not ideal if you are looking for a completely free forever solution, if you sell primarily services or appointments (where other tools work better), or if you need highly complex custom functionality on a very limited budget.

For most Pakistani entrepreneurs selling clothing, accessories, home goods, beauty products, electronics accessories, or dropshipped products, Shopify is the best choice available.

10 Steps to Start Shopify in Pakistan

  1. Start the free trial: Go to shopify.com and sign up for the free trial. No credit card is needed for the first 3 days. After that, the first 3 months are available for $1/month on the promotional plan.
  2. Choose your plan: For most beginners, the Basic plan at $39/month is sufficient. It includes everything you need to run a store — unlimited products, staff accounts, and all standard features.
  3. Pick and install a theme: Shopify's free Dawn theme is excellent for beginners. Browse the Theme Store if you want a premium look. Install the theme and select colors and fonts that match your brand.
  4. Add your products: Go to Products in your admin and add your items with clear titles, descriptions, and high-quality photos. Use variants (size, color) if your products have options.
  5. Set up your domain: Purchase a .com or .pk domain through Shopify or a registrar like GoDaddy. Connect it to your store in Settings > Domains. A custom domain builds trust with customers.
  6. Configure payment gateways: This is the biggest challenge for Pakistani merchants. Stripe is not directly available in Pakistan but can be accessed via a US LLC or Payoneer. Local options include JazzCash, Easypaisa, and Rozarpay. Bank transfer (COD) is widely used.
  7. Set up shipping: Configure shipping zones and rates in Settings > Shipping. For Pakistan, add flat-rate shipping zones for major cities. Connect with TCS, Leopards, or PostEx for logistics.
  8. Add legal pages: Use Shopify's built-in generator to create your privacy policy, refund policy, and terms of service. These are required by payment processors and build customer trust.
  9. Test your checkout: Place a test order using Shopify's test payment method before going live. Verify that confirmation emails send correctly, that your shipping rates calculate properly, and that the checkout works on mobile.
  10. Launch and market: Remove the password page in Online Store > Preferences. Share your store on Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp. Run your first Meta ad campaign and watch your first orders come in.

Common Mistakes Pakistani Beginners Make

  • Installing too many apps immediately: Every app slows your store. Start with the absolute minimum — products, payments, shipping. Add apps only when you have a specific proven need.
  • Skipping the test order: Many beginners launch without testing checkout and discover payment issues only after a real customer tries to buy. Always test first.
  • Using blurry product photos: Pakistan's e-commerce market is growing and customers increasingly expect professional product photography. Low-quality images kill trust and conversions.
  • Not setting up COD properly: Cash on delivery is still the dominant payment method for many Pakistani niches. If COD is not configured correctly, you will lose a large percentage of potential orders.
  • No social proof: New stores without reviews or trust signals convert at very low rates. Even a few genuine customer photos and testimonials on your homepage dramatically improve conversions.

When to Hire a Shopify Developer

Starting Shopify yourself is feasible for a simple product catalog. However, there are clear signals that it's time to bring in a professional:

  • Your store looks noticeably different from professional competitors
  • You're losing sales because of checkout issues or confusing UX
  • You want a custom theme or unique sections that the default theme can't provide
  • Your PageSpeed score is below 50 on mobile
  • You want to integrate with a local logistics API or accounting system
  • You're planning to scale and want the foundation built correctly

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Shopify good for beginners in Pakistan?

Yes, Shopify is one of the best e-commerce platforms for beginners in Pakistan. It requires no coding knowledge, no server management, and no technical maintenance. The admin panel is intuitive, support is available 24/7, and you can start selling within a day. For beginners who want a professional online store without technical complexity, Shopify is the right choice.

How to start Shopify in Pakistan for free?

Shopify offers a 3-day free trial followed by a $1/month for the first 3 months promotional period for new merchants. You can sign up at shopify.com and start building immediately. To accept payments from Pakistan, use Payoneer linked to Stripe, or a local gateway like JazzCash, Easypaisa, or Rozarpay.

How much does it cost to start Shopify in Pakistan?

The minimum cost is approximately $39/month (Basic plan) plus $14/year for a domain. A professional setup with a premium theme and basic apps typically runs $270–$530/month. Hiring a developer for initial setup costs $420–$600 as a one-time fee. Total first-year cost for a basic store runs $1,500–$3,000.
