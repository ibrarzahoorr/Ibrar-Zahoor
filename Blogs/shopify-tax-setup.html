Shopify Tax vs Avalara vs TaxJar

Shopify offers three tiers of tax automation: its own native Shopify Tax service, and integrations with Avalara and TaxJar. Understanding what each provides helps determine the right fit for your store's size, geography, and complexity.

Shopify Tax (Native)

Shopify Tax is built directly into Shopify for US merchants and handles economic nexus tracking, automatic rate updates, and product-category-based tax rules across all 50 US states. It tracks your sales by state and alerts you when you approach nexus thresholds (typically $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions in a state). For stores selling primarily to US customers, Shopify Tax is sufficient for most use cases and costs nothing extra.

When to Use Avalara

Avalara (AvaTax) is the enterprise-grade tax compliance solution — used by most large Shopify Plus merchants. It offers more granular product tax code mapping, multi-country compliance (EU VAT, Canadian GST/HST/PST, Australian GST, Brazilian ICMS), returns filing automation, and exemption certificate management. The added accuracy and compliance features are worth the cost once you're selling at significant volume across multiple US states or multiple countries. Pricing starts around $50–$200/month depending on volume.

When to Use TaxJar

TaxJar sits between Shopify Tax and Avalara in terms of capability and cost. It excels at US sales tax reporting and AutoFile — automatically filing and remitting your state returns on your behalf. If your primary pain point is the compliance filing workload (not just the calculation), TaxJar's AutoFile feature is transformative. Starting at $19/month with AutoFile as an add-on per state.

US Sales Tax — Economic Nexus Rules

The 2018 South Dakota v. Wayfair Supreme Court ruling fundamentally changed US sales tax obligations for online businesses. Previously, you only had sales tax nexus (an obligation to collect) in states where you had a physical presence. Post-Wayfair, most states have economic nexus thresholds that create a collection obligation based purely on sales volume — regardless of where your business is physically located.

Standard Thresholds

The most common threshold is $100,000 in annual sales OR 200 transactions in a state within a calendar year. However, thresholds vary — several states like Kansas have $0 thresholds (nexus from the first sale), while others like Missouri use different calculation periods. Shopify Tax tracks your nexus exposure automatically and alerts you when you're approaching registration thresholds in each state.

When to Register

Register for a sales tax permit in a state before you begin collecting tax there — not after. Operating without a permit while collecting tax is actually illegal in most states. When you reach or approach the nexus threshold in a state, register with the state's Department of Revenue, then configure Shopify Tax to begin collecting for that state. The process is typically online and takes 1–5 business days.

Important: This article provides general information for educational purposes. Sales tax compliance is a legal matter that changes frequently. Always consult a qualified CPA or tax attorney for advice specific to your business situation and the states in which you operate.

EU & UK VAT Setup

European tax compliance adds significant complexity compared to US sales tax. The EU and UK have different systems, different thresholds, and different obligations depending on whether you're selling B2C or B2B, and whether your products are physical goods or digital services.

EU One Stop Shop (OSS) Scheme

The EU OSS scheme, introduced in July 2021, allows EU-based merchants to collect and remit VAT for all EU countries through a single registration in their home country — eliminating the need to register separately in each EU member state. If you're based in the EU and your B2C sales to other EU countries exceed €10,000 per year, OSS registration is required. Non-EU businesses selling to EU customers must register under the Import One Stop Shop (IOSS) for goods under €150, or register for VAT directly in each country for higher-value goods.

UK VAT Post-Brexit

Since Brexit, UK VAT is entirely separate from EU VAT. If you sell to UK customers and your annual UK sales exceed £85,000, you must register for UK VAT, charge the correct rate (20% standard, 5% reduced, 0% zero-rated), file quarterly returns, and remit to HMRC. Shopify's UK market settings handle VAT calculation automatically when configured with your VAT registration number — including the display of prices inclusive or exclusive of VAT based on customer location.

B2B Reverse Charge

For B2B sales within the EU, the VAT reverse charge mechanism means you do not charge VAT to business customers in other EU countries who provide a valid VAT number — instead, the buyer accounts for the VAT in their own country. Shopify handles this through the VAT ID collection field in checkout — when a business customer enters a valid EU VAT number, Shopify automatically applies zero-rate VAT and adds the appropriate tax note to the invoice.

Tax Exemptions & B2B Tax Setup

Managing tax exemptions is one of the most complex and error-prone aspects of Shopify tax configuration. Misconfigured exemptions either result in over-collection (overcharging customers, creating refund obligations) or under-collection (your liability for uncollected tax).

Tax-Exempt Customers

In the US, certain customer types are tax-exempt: government entities, non-profit organizations, and businesses buying for resale (with a valid resale certificate). In Shopify, mark individual customers as tax-exempt in their customer profile — Shopify will then not charge tax on their orders. For higher-volume B2B stores, use Avalara's CertCapture integration to collect, validate, and store exemption certificates digitally.

Configuring Tax Settings for B2B

B2B stores often need to collect VAT registration numbers from business buyers, validate them in real-time, and apply the reverse charge where applicable. Configure Shopify's checkout to display a VAT/Tax ID field for business customers (available in checkout settings). Pair this with an app like B2B/Wholesale Solution or Sufio for invoice generation and VAT validation to automate the B2B tax compliance workflow.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Shopify Tax cost? +
Shopify Tax is included at no extra cost on all Shopify plans for US stores — it handles automatic rate calculation, nexus tracking, and product tax codes for all 50 US states. For international tax automation, TaxJar starts from $19/month and Avalara from approximately $50–$200/month depending on transaction volume. AutoFile (automated returns filing) is an additional cost per state with both third-party services.
How do I set up tax for a store selling to multiple countries? +
Use Shopify Markets to configure separate tax rules per country or region. Each market can have its own VAT/GST registration details, tax rates (entered manually or automated via Avalara), and price display settings (tax-inclusive vs tax-exclusive). For EU merchants, configuring the OSS scheme through your Shopify EU market settings handles multi-country VAT collection through a single registration. Always register in a country before you begin collecting its tax.
Is shipping taxable on Shopify? +
In the US, shipping taxability varies significantly by state. States like California, New York, and Minnesota generally do not tax separately-stated shipping charges, while states like Texas, Florida, and Pennsylvania tax shipping as part of the taxable transaction. Shopify Tax handles this state-by-state automatically. In the EU and UK, shipping is generally subject to VAT at the same rate as the goods being shipped and is included in the taxable amount.
How do I assign the correct product tax code? +
In Shopify admin, go to each product and assign it to the appropriate tax category under the "Shipping" section (e.g., Clothing, Food, Software, Digital Goods). Shopify Tax maps these categories to the correct tax treatment per jurisdiction automatically. This mapping is critical — children's clothing is exempt in New York but not Texas; food items have varying tax status depending on preparation level. Correct categorization is the foundation of accurate tax calculation.
What do I do about tax I should have collected in the past but didn't? +
Retroactive tax compliance — specifically Voluntary Disclosure Agreements (VDAs) — is available in most US states and allows businesses to come forward proactively, pay back taxes owed (often with significant penalty waivers), and get compliant without facing the full liability of an audit. This is entirely a legal and accounting matter. You need a qualified CPA or sales tax attorney to assess your exposure, identify the states where you have uncollected obligations, and manage the VDA process correctly.
