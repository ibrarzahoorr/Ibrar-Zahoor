Canadian Ecommerce Is Cross-Border by Default

Most Shopify advice is written for single-currency, single-market stores. Canadian merchants rarely have that luxury. If you sell home goods, apparel, or specialty products from Toronto, Vancouver, or Montreal, there's a strong chance somewhere between a third and half of your traffic — sometimes more — comes from the US, and you need your store to handle that without friction.

In practice that means:

  • Multi-currency display: Shopify Markets (built into Shopify, or Markets Pro for deeper control) needs to be configured so US visitors see USD pricing at the exchange rate you choose, not raw CAD converted client-side.
  • Duties and customs at checkout: Markets Pro can show landed cost (duty + import tax) at checkout for US-bound orders, which meaningfully reduces the "surprise customs bill" complaints that kill repeat purchases. Setting this up correctly, product by product, is a real project — not a toggle.
  • Fulfillment location strategy: shipping every US order from a Toronto or Mississauga warehouse adds days and customs delays. Many growing Canadian brands split inventory — Canadian fulfillment for CA orders, a US-based 3PL (or Shopify Fulfillment Network alternative) for US orders — which is a fulfillment-app and store-logic decision a developer needs to plan for, not just a shipping setting.
  • GST/HST/PST setup: Canada's tax situation is genuinely more complex than the US — five provinces with HST, others with GST+PST charged separately, Quebec's QST on top. Shopify Tax handles most of this automatically once configured against your nexus, but it needs to be set up against your actual registrations, not left on defaults.

The Timezone Gap, Honestly

This is the one area where Toronto is a tougher fit than the UK or UAE, and it deserves a straight answer rather than a sales pitch. Toronto runs on Eastern Time — UTC-5 (EST) in winter, UTC-4 (EDT) in summer. Pakistan is UTC+5 year-round. That puts Lahore 9-10 hours ahead of Toronto, depending on the season.

What that means concretely: Toronto's 9am start is roughly 6-7pm in Lahore already. There's no way to get a full live overlap with a standard 9-5 schedule on either side — this isn't the UK or UAE situation, and I won't pretend otherwise.

How this actually gets managed:
  1. I shift my working hours later on days with a scheduled Canada call — a fixed daily or weekly sync at, say, 9am Toronto (6-7pm Lahore) covers standups, reviews, and blockers.
  2. The bulk of day-to-day work runs asynchronously: detailed written tickets, Loom walkthroughs of finished work, and same-day written updates land in your morning inbox because they were done during your overnight, my working evening.
  3. Urgent production issues (checkout down, a broken integration) get flagged for a quick evening PKT / morning ET call rather than waiting a full day.

This works well for scoped project work and ongoing retainers where async communication and one daily sync are enough. It's a worse fit if you need someone reachable in real time throughout your entire workday — that's a fair thing to weigh before hiring anyone remotely, not just me.

Why Canadian SMBs Choose Remote Developers Over Local Agencies

Toronto is Canada's most expensive tech labour market, and Shopify agencies there price accordingly — commonly CAD 120-200 an hour, or CAD 900-1,600 for a day-rate engagement. For a growing DTC brand that needs a theme rebuild, a Plus migration, or ongoing app maintenance, that adds up fast, especially once you factor in that Shopify itself (headquartered in Ottawa) has produced a deep local talent pool that agencies compete hard to retain — and price accordingly.

Hiring a remote Shopify Plus developer isn't about avoiding that expertise, it's about not paying Toronto office overhead for it. The actual Liquid, Hydrogen, and Shopify Markets configuration work is identical regardless of where the person doing it sits. What changes is the invoice — a scoped project that a Toronto agency quotes at CAD 6,000+ is realistic at CAD 1,500-2,500 with a remote Shopify Plus developer who's done the same class of work for other international clients.

Do you handle Shopify Markets Pro and duty calculation setup?

Yes — configuring landed cost display, HS codes on product categories, and the CA/US fulfillment split logic is a common project for Canadian clients specifically because of how much cross-border traffic Canadian stores get.

Can you work with an existing Canadian Shopify agency or in-house team?

Regularly — a lot of my Canadian work is a specific slice (a migration, a cross-border setup, a stuck integration) alongside a local team handling marketing or in-person work. I scope around what you already have rather than requiring an all-or-nothing switch.
