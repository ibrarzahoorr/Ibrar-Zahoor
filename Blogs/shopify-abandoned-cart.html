The Recovery Stack: Email + SMS + Retargeting

A single email reminder is not a recovery strategy — it's a missed opportunity. The highest-performing abandoned cart systems operate across three coordinated channels simultaneously, each with its own timing, messaging, and audience targeting.

Email Recovery — Klaviyo Abandoned Cart Flow

Klaviyo's "Started Checkout" trigger fires when a customer provides their email at checkout and then leaves. The three-email sequence (1 hour, 24 hours, 72 hours) captures different segments of abandoners: the distracted, the hesitant, and the price-sensitive. Each email renders dynamic product images, variant details, and a direct cart recovery link that restores the exact cart state.

SMS Recovery — Postscript / SMSBump

SMS messages sent 30–60 minutes after cart abandonment achieve 35–45% open rates — far exceeding email. A single direct SMS ("Hey, you left something behind — grab it here: [link]") often outperforms a three-email sequence for mobile-first audiences. SMS requires explicit opt-in consent and must include opt-out instructions in every message.

Retargeting Ads — Meta & Google

Meta Dynamic Product Ads use your Shopify product catalog to show abandoned cart items in Facebook and Instagram feeds within 30 minutes of abandonment. Google Display retargeting follows the customer across the web. The combination of email + SMS + paid retargeting creates a coordinated "surround sound" recovery effect that drives 3–4x higher recovery rates than any single channel alone.

Channel Coordination: Use Klaviyo's flow suppression feature to stop sending recovery emails to customers who convert via SMS or retargeting ads. Redundant messages after purchase damage brand perception and waste budget. Tag all recovery touchpoints with UTM parameters for accurate attribution in GA4.

Building a High-Converting Cart Recovery Email Sequence

The three-email cart recovery sequence is deceptively simple. The difference between a mediocre 3% recovery rate and an excellent 12% recovery rate lies entirely in execution:

The 3-Email Recovery Sequence

  • Email 1 — 1 Hour After Abandonment
    Subject: "You left something behind..." — Pure product reminder. Large product image, product name, price, direct checkout link. No discount. Conversational, helpful tone. Recovers ~50% of all email-recovered revenue.
  • Email 2 — 24 Hours After Abandonment
    Subject: "Still thinking it over?" — Add social proof: star rating, number of reviews, a genuine customer quote. Introduce scarcity if applicable ("Only 3 left in stock"). Still no discount for most product categories.
  • Email 3 — 72 Hours After Abandonment
    Subject: "Last chance — here's 10% off" — Only for subscribers who haven't purchased or clicked. Present a time-limited discount (10–15%). Include a clear CTA. Suppress from future recovery flows to prevent discount-gaming behavior.

Technical Setup — Tracking & Attribution

The technical foundation for accurate cart recovery tracking requires three components configured correctly:

Shopify Web Pixel: Shopify's native pixel fires the checkout_started event when a customer enters their email at checkout. This event populates Klaviyo's "Started Checkout" metric and triggers the abandonment flow timer. Ensure Shopify's cookie consent is configured to allow marketing tracking under your privacy policy framework.

Klaviyo Active on Site Tracking: The Klaviyo snippet tracks identified (email-known) visitors on product pages, collection pages, and the cart — enabling browse abandonment flows in addition to checkout abandonment. This extends your recovery reach to visitors who add to cart but never reach checkout.

UTM Parameters: Every recovery email link must include UTM parameters: utm_source=klaviyo&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=abandoned_cart&utm_content=email_1. This allows GA4 to attribute recovered revenue to the correct channel and email in your conversion path reports.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a realistic abandoned cart recovery rate? +
A well-configured 3-email Klaviyo sequence typically recovers 5–12% of abandoned checkouts. Adding SMS recovery and retargeting ads can push total recovery to 12–18%. Rates vary by niche — fashion and accessories recover at higher rates than electronics, where purchase decisions are more considered.
Should I offer a discount in cart recovery emails? +
Only in the final email of your sequence (72hr delay), not the first. The first email should simply remind the customer of what they left — most abandons are due to distraction, not price. Offering a discount immediately trains customers to abandon carts deliberately to wait for the coupon, reducing your margin unnecessarily.
Is SMS cart recovery TCPA compliant? +
Yes, but only with explicit prior express written consent from the recipient to receive marketing SMS messages. This consent must be obtained at signup with clear disclosure language — not assumed from a purchase. Postscript and SMSBump provide compliant opt-in tools. Non-compliant SMS marketing carries statutory damages of $500–$1,500 per message under TCPA.
Should I use Shopify's native abandoned checkout emails or Klaviyo? +
Shopify's native abandoned checkout is a single basic email with limited customization. Klaviyo delivers a 3-email sequence with personalized product images, dynamic cart contents, conditional logic, SMS coordination, A/B testing, and detailed revenue attribution. For any store serious about recovery revenue, Klaviyo delivers 3–5x better results.
How does GDPR affect abandoned cart recovery emails in Europe? +
Under GDPR, you can send cart recovery emails to EU subscribers only with marketing consent. However, the "soft opt-in" exception permits sending recovery messages to existing customers about similar products, provided a clear opt-out is included in every email. Always work with a GDPR compliance professional for your specific situation.
