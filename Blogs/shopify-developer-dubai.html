UAE Ecommerce: What's Actually Different

A UAE Shopify store isn't a US or UK store with Arabic text pasted in. There are structural decisions that need to be made correctly from the start:

  • Arabic RTL storefronts: Shopify's Online Store 2.0 themes are built LTR (left-to-right) by default. A genuine bilingual or Arabic-first store needs dir="rtl" logic built into the theme, mirrored icons and layout (cart, navigation, product galleries all flip), and careful checking of any installed app's storefront widgets — many simply weren't built with RTL in mind and need custom CSS overrides to not look broken on an Arabic page.
  • UAE VAT at 5%: lower than the UK or Australia, but still needs to be configured correctly in Shopify Tax against your Federal Tax Authority registration, with tax-inclusive pricing displayed the way UAE consumers expect.
  • Tabby and Tamara: these two Gulf-founded BNPL providers are now closer to default expectations than optional extras for UAE and wider GCC shoppers, similar to how Afterpay functions in Australia. Correct integration means accurate instalment messaging on product pages, not just a checkout-page logo.
  • Cash on delivery (COD): COD remains a meaningfully used payment method in the UAE and broader GCC region compared to the US/UK/Canada, and stores need a checkout flow and fulfilment process that handles COD orders (and their higher return/refusal rate) without falling apart operationally.
  • Local logistics: Aramex and Emirates Post integrations, and same-day/next-day delivery expectations within Dubai and Abu Dhabi specifically, shape which shipping apps make sense.

The Timezone Overlap: About as Good as Remote Gets

The UAE runs on UTC+4 year-round (no daylight saving). Pakistan is UTC+5 year-round. That's a one-hour gap — Lahore is simply one hour ahead of Dubai, all year, no seasonal shifting to track.

What a 1-hour gap means in practice:
  1. A 9am start in Dubai is 10am in Lahore — both teams are online and working within the same hour.
  2. The entire UAE business day (roughly 9am-6pm Gulf time, given the region's Sunday-Thursday work week in many sectors) overlaps almost completely with a standard Pakistani working day.
  3. Live calls, same-morning Slack replies, and same-day fixes work exactly like they would with an in-country hire — there's no schedule gymnastics required on either side.
  4. The Sunday-Thursday work week common across much of the UAE is also standard practice for many Pakistan-based freelancers serving Gulf clients, which removes another potential friction point around which days are "working days."

Of every region covered in this series — UK, Canada, Australia — Dubai is the one where the timezone genuinely stops being a factor in how the working relationship runs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Shopify actually do a proper Arabic RTL store, or does it need a workaround?

Shopify itself doesn't auto-flip a theme to RTL — it needs deliberate theme development: dir="rtl" templates, mirrored UI elements, and testing every installed app's frontend widgets for RTL breakage. It's absolutely achievable, but it's real development work, not a language-pack toggle.

Do you set up Tabby and Tamara, or just install the app?

Both apps handle the payment logic themselves once installed, but getting instalment messaging to show correctly on product and cart pages, and making sure they don't visually clash with COD or card options at checkout, is the part that actually needs a developer's attention.

Is the Sunday-Thursday work week an issue for ongoing support?

No — it's simple to align schedules around Gulf business days for UAE clients specifically, since it doesn't conflict with how I already structure work weeks for other Pakistan-based clients.
