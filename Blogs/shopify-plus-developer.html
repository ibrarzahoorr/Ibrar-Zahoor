What Shopify Plus Offers vs Basic and Advanced

Shopify Plus is the enterprise tier of Shopify, designed for merchants processing high volumes, managing complex B2B operations, or needing more control over the checkout experience. While Shopify Basic and Advanced provide an excellent foundation for most stores, Plus unlocks a set of features that justify its higher subscription cost for the right business.

The key Plus features that require developer expertise to implement are not self-service — they need someone who understands Shopify's architecture deeply enough to configure B2B portals, write Shopify Scripts in Ruby, build Flow automations, and extend the checkout with Checkout Extensions. Ibrar Zahoor has hands-on Plus experience from working with major clients including ZARR, a fashion brand serving over 70 million users.

B2B Wholesale Features

Shopify Plus includes native B2B functionality that allows you to create wholesale storefronts with customer-specific pricing, minimum order quantities, payment terms (Net 30/60), draft order workflows, and company/location account structures. Setting this up correctly requires understanding how Shopify's B2B APIs work and how to structure your customer data, pricing rules, and catalog visibility.

Ibrar Zahoor configures Shopify Plus B2B portals from scratch — company account structure, payment terms, pricing lists, metafield-driven catalog restrictions, and custom B2B checkout flows. If your business sells to both retail and wholesale customers, the Plus B2B setup allows you to serve both from a single Shopify organization without managing two separate stores.

Launchpad for Sales Events

Launchpad is a Plus-exclusive tool for scheduling and automating sales events — flash sales, product drops, seasonal promotions. With Launchpad, you schedule price changes, theme changes, collection visibility, and inventory holds to activate and deactivate automatically at exact times. This is critical for brands running high-traffic product launches where manual changes during peak traffic would be risky and slow.

Shopify Flow Automation

Shopify Flow is Plus's no-code automation engine — think Zapier but built natively into Shopify with direct access to order, product, customer, and inventory data. Ibrar Zahoor builds Flow workflows for high-value automation use cases: tagging customers based on lifetime value, flagging high-risk orders for review, sending restock notifications, triggering fulfillment holds, and automating loyalty tier updates.

Scripts for Custom Checkout Logic

Shopify Scripts (and the newer Checkout Extensions) allow developers to write Ruby code that runs at checkout — applying custom discount logic, hiding/showing shipping options based on cart contents, reordering payment methods, and building custom gift logic. This level of checkout customization is only available on Plus, and it requires developer expertise to implement correctly.
