Shopify Plus B2B — What It Actually Includes

For many years, Shopify's B2B capabilities lagged behind dedicated wholesale platforms like Handshake or OrderEase. That changed significantly with Shopify Plus's native B2B feature set, which now handles the most important wholesale scenarios without requiring third-party apps. Understanding what's available natively versus what requires custom development is critical before starting a B2B project.

Shopify Plus B2B is available exclusively on the Plus plan (starting at $2,300/month). It provides a separate B2B storefront that can live on a subdomain, powered by the same Shopify backend as your D2C store. Wholesale buyers get their own login, see their assigned pricing, and experience a completely separate (or shared) checkout flow. This solves one of the oldest wholesale headaches: hiding retail pricing from wholesale buyers and vice versa.

Customer-Specific Pricing

The cornerstone of B2B commerce is differentiated pricing. Different buyers get different prices — a major retailer buying 500 units per order pays differently from a small boutique buying 50. Shopify Plus handles this through Price Lists, which let you assign specific fixed prices or percentage discounts to individual company accounts or customer segments.

Price Lists in Shopify Plus support: fixed price overrides per product variant, percentage discounts off the standard price, currency-specific pricing for international wholesale buyers, and quantity breaks (volume pricing). These are assigned at the company or location level, so a single company can have multiple locations (warehouses, regional offices) each with their own pricing or payment terms.

Shopify B2B Features Checklist

  • Company accounts with multiple locations and contacts
  • Customer-specific price lists (fixed or percentage)
  • Volume pricing / quantity breaks
  • Net payment terms (Net 7, 15, 30, 60, 90)
  • Purchase order (PO) number field at checkout
  • Order approval workflows
  • Bulk order forms (CSV import or quick-order grid)
  • Saved draft orders for sales rep use
  • Tax exemption settings per company
  • Separate B2B storefront with B2B-only products
  • Company account self-service portal
  • Net terms invoice management

Net Payment Terms

Net payment terms are a requirement for serious B2B commerce. A buyer ordering $10,000 of product doesn't pay at checkout — they expect to pay within 30 days of invoice receipt. Shopify Plus now supports Net 7, Net 15, Net 30, Net 60, and Net 90 terms natively. When a buyer with net terms places an order, no payment is collected at checkout. Instead, Shopify generates an invoice with the due date, and the buyer pays via the payment link when ready.

Custom Order Forms & Quote Requests

Most B2B buyers need more than a standard product page with an add-to-cart button. They need to order multiple SKUs simultaneously, request custom pricing on large orders, or submit RFQ (request for quote) forms. Ibrar builds:

  • Quick-order grid forms — a table view of all products with quantity inputs for rapid bulk ordering
  • CSV bulk order upload — buyers upload their own SKU/quantity spreadsheet
  • Quote request forms — buyers submit large orders for approval before payment
  • Draft order management — sales reps create orders on behalf of customers
  • Minimum order quantity (MOQ) enforcement per product or per order

Bulk Ordering UI

The default Shopify product page is designed for retail — one product at a time, one variant at a time. Wholesale buyers find this infuriating. They need to order 12 units of size S, 24 of size M, and 18 of size L in a single form submission. Ibrar builds custom bulk ordering interfaces using Shopify Liquid and JavaScript: variant matrix tables that show all size/color combinations with individual quantity inputs, a running order total, and a single add-to-cart action that places all variants simultaneously. These UX improvements directly reduce the friction that causes B2B buyers to abandon orders.

Pro Tip: B2B buyers reorder frequently. Adding a "reorder from previous order" feature to the customer account portal can increase repeat order rate by 30–50% for wholesale clients. Ibrar builds this as a custom Liquid component connected to the Orders API.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Shopify support B2B? +
Yes. Shopify Plus includes native B2B features including company accounts, customer-specific pricing (price lists), net payment terms, purchase order fields, and separate B2B storefronts. For standard Shopify plans, B2B features can be added via apps like Wholesale Club or Wholesale Gorilla, though these are less robust than the native Plus features.
What is Shopify B2B? +
Shopify B2B is a suite of features in Shopify Plus that enables wholesale and business-to-business selling. It includes company accounts with multiple locations and contacts, customer-specific price lists, net payment terms (Net 30, 60, etc.), purchase order support, and a dedicated B2B storefront separate from your retail store. It allows brands to serve both retail and wholesale customers from a single Shopify backend.
How do I add wholesale pricing to Shopify? +
On Shopify Plus, you add wholesale pricing through Price Lists in the B2B section of the admin. You create a price list with fixed prices or percentage discounts and assign it to a company account. On standard Shopify plans, you can use apps like Wholesale Club to create customer tag-based pricing tiers. Ibrar configures both approaches depending on your Shopify plan and pricing complexity.
